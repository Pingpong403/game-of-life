The Game of Life

Read below before pressing play!

Conway's Game of Life is a simulation of a simple, microscopic ecosystem. This ecosystem consists of living and dead cells. Living cells appear in white, while dead cells appear in black.

Living cells can die if they're suffocated (more than 3 neighbors) or lonely (less than 2). Dead cells can revive if they have exactly 3 neighbors (like reproduction).

The game progresses in generations! The rules above apply to cells changing from one generation to the next.

This puzzle is meant to simulate the importance of starting conditions. Your target is a nice, healthy ecosystem that maintains its mass and therefore lives on forever in harmony.

This ecosystem currently has a tiny problem: it is missing a cell! This may seem trivial, but lacking a single cell is what's causing the ecosystem to eventually fail. Stagnation is death.

Figure out where the single cell belongs. Once you do, you unlock full interactivity!
